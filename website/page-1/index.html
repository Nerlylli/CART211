f5ve - Underground
Despite the upbeat melody, the song ‘Underground’ by f5ve, has a deep message about the social pressures brought on by capitalism, making people want to withdraw from the outside world and live in the underground. In the song, that place is used as a metaphor, both for capitalism, as well as a critique of it. At first, the metro – a public transit system frequently utilized by the working class to commute – serves as a representation of the underground world they are trying to escape. Later on in the video, that same underground world transforms into a rave, and into a subterranean automobile meet, which is where they feel free.

Capitalism is the source of many issues.
For example, the digital spying that takes place on practically every platform on our devices.

Majority of them are free.

We use the internet and register on social media sites with the hope that our privacy will be protected by using our email address, and a unique password for each site.

But the true question is: How do these large corporations still manage to generate revenue?
Although being free to use, they managed to continue operating for so long; how?

The answer, our data.

Google makes money by knowing where we are, where we are going, who our connections are, and more.

Similar companies compete for our attention, accomplishing this through the development of interest-based algorithms and technical advancements.

In short, in a capitalist society, nothing is free.
If you are not paying for the product, you are the product.
