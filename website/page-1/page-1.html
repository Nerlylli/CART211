f5ve - Underground
Despite the upbeat melody, the song ‘Underground’ by f5ve, has a deep message about the social pressures brought on by capitalism, making people want to withdraw from the outside world and live in the underground. In the song, that place is used as a metaphor, both for capitalism, as well as a critique of it. At first, the metro – a public transit system frequently utilized by the working class to commute – serves as a representation of the underground world they are trying to escape. Later on in the video, that same underground world transforms into a rave, and into a subterranean automobile meet, which is where they feel free.
