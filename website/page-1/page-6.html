Imagination is not something that we learn; rather, it is something that we,
as humans, tend to lose over time because of school, work, and other
obligations. For this reason, art is crucial to society. In a world full of social
norms, art enables people to challenge them by self-expressing themselves
in so many ways. That is why the use of AI is in no way ethical, especially
in this specific field. However, because its use seems to be becoming
somewhat inevitable, it should be regulated, as the boundaries between
inspiration and blatant plagiarism sometimes blur. Ai is continuously evolving.
With its rapid updates comes the fear of it replacing humans in many fields.
However, the problem does not only lie within its presence, but rather the
ways in which people use it, often to prompt things that could’ve easily been
done by humans. In fact, AI imitates and spits out an artificially generated
version, using the tools and stylistic elements that artists spend their
lives developing and coming up with. AI opens the door to commodify these
things, making them faster and cheaper. The ways in which some interact with
this tool have caused us to become impatient, leading us to expect instant
results and gratification, underestimating the necessity of the process. In the
field of art, however, it is not always the piece itself that is interesting or
impressive, but the story behind it. What is it about? What is the piece
portraying? What does it say about the time period, about the artist? Art is a
way to connect with one another, free of language barriers and time spaces.
In fact, art was humans’ first language.AI is a product of capitalism. To support
the development of AI, is to oppose our own growth.
